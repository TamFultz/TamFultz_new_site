About me

I am a museum librarian experienced in complex cataloging and knowledgable about metadata. At the Thomas J. Watson Library, I catalog the special collections materials such as artists' books and fine publishers' bindings. As an adjunct faculty member, I teach an online course for Rutgers University School of Communication and information entitled Metadata for the Information Professional. Living in Brooklyn for over 20 years, I'm an avid brunch addict. I love everything in regards to art, literature, and the obscure. I share an abode with the ever-enigmatic Vivian Darkbloom and two neurotic cats, Leon and Diego (named after the "friends" Leon Trotsky and Diego Rivera).
